## 1. Comcast Internet — $89.99/month

### Strategy: Competitor Leverage + Retention Threat

**Research first:**
- Check T-Mobile Home Internet pricing at your address: $50/month
- Check AT&T Fiber pricing: $55–$65/month
- Check Verizon Fios pricing: $39.99–$49.99/month

### Script:

> "Hi, I've been a Comcast customer for [X] years and I need to talk about my bill. I'm currently paying $89.99/month for internet and I've received offers from T-Mobile Home Internet for $50/month and AT&T for $55/month. I really don't want to switch, but I need my bill to be more competitive. What can you do for me?"

**If they offer a small discount:**
> "I appreciate that, but $50/month is the number I need to hit. Can you connect me with your retention department? I'll need to make a decision today."

**Escalation to Retention:**
> "I'd like to submit a cancellation request. Before I do, is there anything the retention team can offer to keep my business?"

**Expected outcome:** $20–$40/month reduction or a promotional rate at $49.99–$64.99/month for 12 months.

**Best time to call:** Weekday mornings (9–11am) — shorter hold times, more experienced agents.

**Annual Savings: $300–$480**

## 3. New York Times — $29.99/month ⭐ EASIEST WIN

### Strategy: Cancellation → Retention Offer

**This is your easiest $20+/month win.** NYT's retention team routinely offers 80–90% discounts to prevent cancellation.

### Script (online or by phone):

**Online:** Go to NYT.com → Account → Cancel Subscription → Follow the cancellation flow. They will present retention offers automatically.

**By phone (1-800-698-4637):**
> "Hi, I need to cancel my New York Times subscription. I'm currently paying $29.99/month and I can't justify that cost anymore."

**They will offer a discount. Common retention offers:**
- $4/month for 12 months (87% savings)
- $8/month for 6 months (73% savings)
- 3 months free, then discounted rate

> "Can you offer me something closer to $4–$5/month? That's what I've seen others get as a retention offer."

**Accept any offer under $10/month.** You can cancel and re-subscribe after the promotion expires to reset the clock.

**Estimated Savings: $22–$26/month ($264–$312/year)**

## 7. Credit Card APR Negotiation

### Strategy: Request Rate Reduction Based on Payment History

**Script (call the number on the back of your Visa card):**

> "Hi, I've been a [Bank] customer for [X] years and I have an excellent payment history. I'm calling to request a reduction in my APR. My current rate is [X]%. I've received pre-approval offers from other cards at [lower rate]% and I'd prefer to keep my business here. Can you lower my rate?"

**What to have ready:**
- Current APR (find on your statement)
- Competitor card offer with lower rate (Chase, Capital One, Citi all mail offers)
- Your credit score (if 700+, you have strong leverage)

**Expected outcome:** 2–6% APR reduction, which can save $200–$600/year if carrying a balance.

### Action Steps:

2. **If you don't recognize it — Dispute immediately:**

   - The credit card company must investigate within **30 days**
   - You are **not responsible** for unauthorized charges

4. **If it's a forgotten subscription:**

   - Request a refund for the most recent charge
